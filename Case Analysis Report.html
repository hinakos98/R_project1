Charles Book Club Case Analysis
The Charles Book Club (CBC), established in December 1986, aimed to differentiate
itself by understanding its customer base and offering tailored products. Despite increasing
mailing volume and customer database size, CBC faced declining profits. To address this, they
planned to utilize database marketing to target profitable customers and design tailored
marketing strategies. In this scenario, CBC is attempting to send out mailings to gauge interest in
a book titled "The Art History of Florence" while minimizing costs by targeting only potential
buyers, highlighting the importance of effective targeting in maximizing profitability.
Analysis
Database marketing utilizes segmentation to group prospects into homogeneous segments
based on their buying behavior. The goal is to identify individuals with a high propensity to
purchase a particular offering. Since this propensity can't be directly measured, variables such as
R; recency (time since last purchase), F; frequency (number of previous purchases from the
company over a period), and M; monetary value (amount of money spent on the company’s
products over a period) are commonly used indicators. These variables, known as RFM
variables, help businesses effectively target potential customers in direct marketing campaigns.

Logistic Regression
I created a logistic regression model using the code below. For logistic regression, we
have to use a binary variable. I wanted to analyze the logistic regression of recency (time since
last purchase) and whether customers purchased Art History of Florence which is categorized as
yes or no (binary). This code below creates a scatter plot of the data points with a smoothed
curve fitted using a generalized linear model assuming a binomial distribution.
ggplot(flo_sum, aes(x = R,
y = prop_flo,
weight = count)) +
geom_point() +
geom_smooth(method = "glm",
se = FALSE,
method.args = list(family = "binomial"))
Chart 3 is the result. The prop_flo axis is the binary variable (yes= 1, no= 0), and the label for the
R axis is the month since last purchased. From this chart, we can predict that customers who
shop more often are more likely to buy Art History of Florence.
